footle v. [FOO-tul]

vi. 胡闹；浪费(时间)
n. 愚蠢；胡话

Definition

1: to talk or act foolishly
1：愚蠢地说话或行为

2: to waste time: trifle, fool
2：浪费时间：浪费，鬼混

注：trifle：n. 琐事，小事，v. 浪费，玩弄；fool：n. 傻瓜，小丑，v. 愚弄，欺骗，玩弄，鬼混

Did You Know?

Footle will be more familiar to speaker of British English than it is to speaker of American English. Its likely source is the seldom-used footer, meaning “to waste time.” That word is etymologically connected with fouter (also spelled foutra), a word referring to something of little value or someone worthless or bungling. But the link between footle and footer is speculative. What we can say with confidence is that footle is a verb of 19th century origin that — along with the derivative adjective footling (as in “a footling amateur”) — is still apt when discussing foolish or trifling people or things.

词源词根演化：(推测的) fouter (“sth. of little value”) —> footer (“to waste time”) —> footle (v.)

相比起美式英语世界，英式英语世界可能对footle更加熟悉。这个词可能来自于一个极少使用的单词footer，意思是“浪费时间”。从词源上看，footer可能与fouter （或 foutra） ，表示“没有价值的东西”或者“笨拙的或者无用的人”，有些联系。我们有信心下的结论是：footle是起源于19世纪的动词 — 还包括一个衍生的形容词形式 footling — 当提及愚蠢的或者无用的人物时，依然可以使用这个词。

注：bungling：笨拙的，粗制滥造的；

Examples

下面这两个例子，都来自于英式英语文章。

  • “A very British invention …, the steam locomotive, derived as it was from [George] Stephenson’s nippy Rocket machine on parallel tracks, and the experiments of a young boy called James Watt, who footled about with a spoon in the steam from his aunt’s kettle.”
    — John Lewis-Stempel, The Express Online (U.K.), 5 Aug. 2018

    一个真正的英国发明…，蒸汽火车头，衍生自[George] Stephenson (乔治 史蒂芬孙)的运行在平行铁轨上的快速火箭汽车，以及James Watt的试验，这个年轻小伙子把一把勺子放在她婶婶的烧水壶冒出的蒸汽里打发时间。

    注：nipppy：刺骨的，锐利的，（狗）爱咬人的；

  • “I left the convent an ability to curtsey, two rubbish A-levels and a large repertoire of slightly scandalous songs. I footled around for a year, wasted three years at university, footled around a bit more and then finally did what I’d wanted to do all along — go to drama school… .”
    — Dillie Keane, The Guardian (London), 19 Aug. 2015

    我从女修院学校毕业，学会了屈膝礼，还有两个垃圾级别的A-level文凭与一个有点诽谤性歌曲的大剧目。我先浪费了一年时间，又在大学里混了三年，之后又游荡了一段时间，终于开始干我一直都想从事的事情 — 上戏曲学院。

    注：convent：女修道院，女修院开办的学校；curtsey：（也作 curtsy）屈膝礼；repertoire：剧目；scandalous：诽谤性的
